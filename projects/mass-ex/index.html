About the Show

In its simplicity and durability, SLATE serves as a pristine canvas, presented as a base, and a starting point devoid of excess. We intend to use this base as an invitation for the students to inscribe their narratives upon it. This event aims to offer a refined platform where the essential qualities of slate inspire creativity and personal interpretation.

In providing this canvas, we are careful not to impose a vision too complex, ensuring that the essence of SLATE—its raw, fundamental nature—keeps the student’s work as the star of the show above all else. Students are encouraged to project their stories and perspectives, utilizing the next-to-blank slate to support and display their concepts. The show’s design will be stripped to its core and polished to excellence, leaving room for the diversity of ideas that designers will bring to the surface.

What is Mass Exodus?

Mass Exodus is the School of Fashion’s annual end-of-year production that celebrates and showcases the achievements and creativity of final-year Fashion students at Toronto Metropolitan University. With a new and updated curriculum, our fashion students will be exploring different concentrations across Design, Communication, Design Leadership, Fashion Studies, and Material and Textile Practices.

Mass Exodus aims to merge the student ideas and the greater fashion community in Toronto together as a way to carve out paths for their future careers.

Mass Exodus is brought to life by the hardworking team of students in the Live Events Supercourse. This class is one of the three experimental Supercourses offered by The Creative School that bridges students across a variety of creative programs to learn and work together to produce, manage, and promote the multi-stakeholder fashion and cultural event that is Mass Exodus.

Master Document / Assets

As creative direction lead it was crucial to my role that I established a concrete visual direction for the encompassing team. I created the master document for ease of access, and to utilize for a cohesive look and feel.

Included all primary elements, the most notable being color swatches, typefaces, and textures to be utilized across all platforms.

Logo + 3d Iteration

Collaborating with fellow Branding & Design Department members, we crafted the events logo by adapting the standard variant into a stamped iteration, in line with our theme's raw and stripped-back aesthetics.

General Posters

Using a combination of Illustrator and Photoshop, I created the general posters for the event with provided raw photography from the image + video department.

Initial Communication Gaps

There were notable challenges in communication among the executives early in the semester, leading to misalignments and occasional confusion that affected the initial planning stages.

Better regulated meetings

My team could have benefited from more substantial and regulated meetings. We started strong, during our most pressing times, but as the semester went on we definitely loosened up on the necessity of meetings outside of class. I wouldn’t say it affected our final deliverables or overall productivity but it could have been a good opportunity to boost morale and camaraderie earlier and even stronger than we ended with.

07 / 08

Installation

Build the space, test technical systems, rehearse, brief the team, resolve issues, and prepare for opening.

CreativeFest Reflection

The CreativeFest, whilst admittedly something initially seeming like an obstruction to our production, ended up being an exceptional opportunity to explore ideas of arrangement that would be later used for Mass Exodus. Serving as somewhat of a trial period for all aspects of the event - set up, takedown, and operation - it was a great chance to ensure the various aspects would be understood on the day.

My role was Curator, a part of the set-up phase; a phenomenal opportunity to utilize my primary role of Creative Direction Lead to the material aspects of the event.

We were particularly pleased with the draping over the wooden logo paired with the muslin pipe and drapes; something we would go on to replicate with equal effectiveness for the Mass Exodus runway.

Overall I would say this was a great opportunity to understand and get prepared for the official show, establishing the team dynamics, technical considerations, and overall flow of event set-up.

Event / Reflection

Run the event, document the experience, gather feedback, evaluate outcomes, and identify what should be retained or improved.

Event Reflection

In regards to the day of the event, I can’t imagine it going any smoother from the team's perspective, everyone was willing to help, was focused, and most importantly had a great time.

Overall, the journey of creating this year's Mass Exodus will stay with me for the rest of my life. It was an incredible opportunity, elevated by an incredible team. Moving forward, I believe this journey has equipped me with substantial knowledge to pursue this avenue of work further, as well as translate it into any additional avenues I may find myself in.

Event Takeaways

Teamwork and Bonding

The strong camaraderie and mutual investment among the team members created a supportive environment that was pivotal to the success of the event.

Leadership and Creative Direction

Taking the role of Creative Direction Lead allowed for significant personal growth in leadership and establishing a cohesive visual and narrative direction for the event, which exceeded personal expectations.

Focused Vision on Student Work

The decision to center the event around the students' work helped in creating an unobstructed and inclusive presentation, enhancing the overall impact and reception of the event.

Initial Communication Gaps

There were notable challenges in communication among the executives early in the semester, leading to misalignments and occasional confusion that affected the initial planning stages.

Volunteer Coordination

During the event, there were instances where volunteer coordination was not optimal, leading to disorganization and underutilization of resources at times.

Enhanced Volunteer Coordination

There’s an opportunity to improve how volunteers are coordinated and managed to ensure that their skills and numbers are utilized efficiently throughout the event, not just during peak times.
